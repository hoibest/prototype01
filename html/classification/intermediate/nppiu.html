Classification Criteria for
Intermediate Uveitis, Non-Pars Planitis Type
Should satisfy #1 AND #2

  1. Evidence of intermediate uveitis
    1. Vitreous cells AND/OR vitreous haze
    2. If anterior chamber cells are present, anterior chamber inflammation severity less than vitreous severity
    3. No evidence of retinitis

  2. No evidence of pars planitis
    1. Vitreous snowballs NOR
    2. Pars plana snowbanks

Exclude if any criterion below is fulfilled

  • Multiple sclerosis, defined by the McDonald criteria
  • Positive serology for syphilis using a treponemal test
  • Evidence of sarcoidosis (either bilateral hilar adenopathy on chest imaging or tissue biopsy demonstrating non-caseating granulomata)
  • Positive serology for Lyme disease, either IgG or IgM (e.g. positive ELISA AND Western blot with requisite number of bands for assay used)
  • Evidence of intraocular lymphoma on diagnostic vitrectomy
